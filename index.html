Over the course of almost seven years, I have held various roles, from hotel sales representative to project coordinator. I enjoyed my work, but I was not fulfilled by my career choice.

About three years ago, almost by accident, I began learning CSS while reading a book my brother, who is a web designer, left in my house. Having recently moved from California to Spain, I was looking for a change in my career.

The book was so fascinating to me, that while reading, I searched online for exercises for practicing. Throughout the book, the author recommended Codecademy, while I found FreeCodeCamp recommendations on Reddit.

Their curriculum was well-structured. It was light enough that anyone without programming experience could use it. However, the basic courses were not very realistic, as they held my hand every step of the way. They were good enough to start, but real-life programming rarely works that way.

I spent a lot of time learning the foundations: CSS, HTML, and Javascript, especially Javascript, because even if you use the fanciest frameworks or libraries, the World Wide Web was built upon these technologies.

As things got tough, I had a lot of doubts and feared I couldn't learn to program; but as time passed, getting stuck and trying to find solutions in different ways became second nature to me.

I got used to feeling uncomfortable, I worked hard and persevered. KinderBday

Together with four other developers, I created an app where the host of a party, usually a children's one, can share with guests what the child wants as a gift; therefore, it can be a more meaningful present for the child.

We fell in love with the idea instantly, I hate to see all the waste of resources of the toy industry, the most plastic intensive industry in the world.
Using MySQL and Node.JS on the back end, and React, Bootstrap and SASS on the front end, we created a beautiful MVP.
Within a period of two months, we were assigned different tasks on one-week sprints, using the Agile methodology and project-managed by space-pilots.
